Rating philosophy

A balanced self-assessment starts from evidence

3

3 is strong performance

A 3 means you consistently met expectations for your role and delivered reliable results. It is the standard for strong, fully effective performance.

4+

Higher ratings need sustained stretch

A 4 or 5 should be supported by repeated examples of stronger execution, initiative, broader influence, or materially elevated outcomes.

E

Evidence matters more than volume

A strong self-assessment explains what happened, why it mattered, and how your contribution connects to role expectations and the driver.

Six key drivers

Driver-by-driver self-reflection

Each driver includes self-reflection questions, first-person rating anchors, and evidence prompts to help you prepare a fair, specific self-assessment.

Self-assessment process

How to choose and explain your rating

Use the rating anchors as a reflection tool. The goal is to prepare a clear, honest view of your performance that supports a better review conversation.

1

Start with role expectations

Review your goals, responsibilities, role level, and any expectations agreed with your manager before choosing a rating.

2

Use 3 as the anchor

Ask whether you consistently met expectations for the driver. If yes, 3 may be the right base before testing whether evidence supports 4 or 5.

3

Test up or down

Move higher only when your examples show sustained stretch beyond expectations. Move lower when important expectations were inconsistent or required significant support.

4

Write the evidence

For each driver, capture a short example with the situation, your action, the result, and what changed because of your contribution.

If you are between two ratings: choose the lower anchor unless the higher rating is supported by sustained, role-relevant evidence across the review period. Use the review conversation to discuss any uncertainty.

Writing guidance

Make your self-assessment useful

Evidence expectations

Bring examples that show sustained performance

Strong evidence is specific, connected to your role, and clear about the outcome or behaviour it demonstrates.

Specific

Describe the situation, your action, the outcome, and the timeframe.

Balanced

Include achievements, learning, constraints, and areas you want to improve.

Sustained

Look for patterns across the review period, not one recent or visible example.

Role-relevant

Connect your examples to expectations for your current role level and driver.
